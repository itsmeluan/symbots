Part icon: Reinforced Servo Arm (Scrapjaw, Rare, ARMS slot, Kinetic).
Canvas 256x256 px. 3/4 view, part isolated on transparent background.

DESCRIPTION: "The Servo Arm, welded over until it stopped breaking."
This is the Servo Arm (scrapjaw_servo_arm) evolved — the same arm, but with visible
reinforcement welds, additional plating layered over the original, and reinforced
structural nodes at the stress points. It reads as the same arm, improved by iteration
rather than redesign. Should share general proportions with the Servo Arm but with
clearly added mass and detail.

VISUAL DIFFERENTIATION FROM SERVO ARM:
- Additional armor plates welded over the original surface (visible layering)
- Reinforcement welds at the elbow joint and mount end (thick bead welds)
- Slightly more mass — the arm looks heavier and more durable
- More prominent diagonal slash panel-lines (the reinforcement added new panel geometry)
- A visible reinforced attachment clasp (stronger than the basic quick-release)

ARMS SLOT SHAPE LANGUAGE: Same as servo arm — extends laterally, mid-zone. WEAPON
attachment point at hand end (flat or slotted mounting face). CHASSIS mount end with
panel-line continuation seam.

SCRAPJAW FINISH: Heavy diagonal slash panel-lines across ALL surfaces including
the added reinforcement panels. Multiple layers of weld marks. The "welded over until
it stopped breaking" concept is readable on the surface — you can almost count the
repair generations.

KINETIC ELEMENT: Silver-shift #D8DDE6 on forearm and impact nodes, polished chrome
on reinforced stress areas. Impact Ring glyph on forearm face.

RARITY FRAME (Rare): Double border (#4B5668 outer + #374350 inner) + diamond pip
in corner. Soft ambient silver Kinetic glow at ~25% opacity around the part shape.

GREYSCALE TEST: Distinguishable from the basic Servo Arm by visible added mass/layering
at 64x64px.

NEGATIVE: No clean factory surface (this is layered scrap repairs). No photoreal.
No painterly gradients. No rounded UI frame.
