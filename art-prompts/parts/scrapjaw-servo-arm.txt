Part icon: Servo Arm (Scrapjaw, Common, ARMS slot, Kinetic).
Canvas 256x256 px. 3/4 view, part isolated on transparent background.

DESCRIPTION: "The arm every Symbot starts with. Reliable, replaceable."
A basic mechanical arm — simple, functional, no frills. This is the entry-level ARMS part.
It should look like the mechanical arm of a bot: a forearm-and-hand unit with a simple
gripper or flat-ended hand. Proportioned to fit the mid-zone lateral slot of a bot.
Not a weapon — it is the ARMS slot itself, the carrier for WEAPON attachments.

ARMS SLOT SHAPE LANGUAGE (art-bible §3.3): Extends laterally; sits in the mid-zone
(never above the CHASSIS top line); may have a flat attachment point at the "hand" end
where a WEAPON would clip on. The seam at the CHASSIS-end shows a continuation panel
line (attachment grammar). Wide enough to read as "the shoulder-to-hand extent" of a bot.

SCRAPJAW FINISH: Diagonal ~45-degree slash panel-lines across the forearm surface.
Quick-release clasp visible at the mount end (CHASSIS-side). Asymmetric detail — the
arm looks like it was adapted from something else, not precision-machined. Weld marks
at the seam areas. Simple but functional.

KINETIC ELEMENT: Silver-shift #D8DDE6 on the forearm surface and contact nodes.
Impact Ring glyph (concentric circles) subtly present on the forearm face.
Matte structural highlights in Bone White #F2EDDF on the arm frame.

RARITY FRAME (Common): Single #374350 Slate Gunmetal border. No glow. Clean and plain.
The most unremarkable frame treatment — deliberately so.

GREYSCALE TEST: Reads as an arm/limb shape at 64x64px. The CHASSIS-mount end and
WEAPON-mount end are distinguishable by their geometry.

NEGATIVE: No legs shape. No head shape. No weapon integrated (this is an arm carrier,
not a weapon). No photoreal. No painterly gradients. No rounded UI frame.
