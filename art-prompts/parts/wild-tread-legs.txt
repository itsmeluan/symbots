Part icon: Tread Legs (Wild, Common, LEGS slot, Kinetic).
Canvas 256x256 px. Front-facing or slight 3/4 low view, part isolated on transparent background.

DESCRIPTION: "Salvaged treads. Slow to turn, hard to tip."
Wild-origin LEGS that use tank-tread locomotion instead of bipedal legs. Salvaged
tread units — wide, stable, low-to-ground. Heavy and planted. The tread housings show
organic weathering and wild surface adaptation but still carry the engineered attachment
nodes at the top (where they mount to the CHASSIS).

LEGS SLOT SHAPE LANGUAGE (art-bible §3.3):
- Ground-contact element — the bot's foundation
- Widest or most complex LOWER element of the bot
- Must NEVER extend above the CHASSIS base line — owns the lower zone entirely
- Defense/heavy archetype: wide, planted, squat, multiple ground contacts
- "Mass-drop rule": LEGS carry more visual weight at the bottom — gives the bot gravity

TREAD LEGS SPECIFIC FORM:
Two wide tread units (one left, one right) viewed from front-and-slightly-below. Each
tread unit is a rectangular housing containing a tank-track circuit — the upper housing
body and the track wrapping around it visible at the bottom. The tread housings are
wide and squat (low, hard-to-tip quality). CHASSIS mount at the top of each housing:
a shared connection crossbar or individual mount brackets showing the engineered
attachment geometry.

The "slow to turn" quality reads in the silhouette: the treads are optimized for
forward traction, not rotation — wide and straight-tracked, not articulated for agile
turns. Heavy visual weight at the bottom of each housing (the track ground-contact area).

WILD MANUFACTURER FINISH:
- Tread housings have organic weathered surfaces — mineral deposits, dirt/debris
  caught in tread gap areas, rust-and-growth patina on the housing tops
- The tread material itself has a slightly organic texture (rubber or composite that
  looks partially grown, not factory-stamped)
- Vegetation threading: faint suggestion of root or vine material in the tread gap
  (biome-adaptive wild character, Vegetation zone lean)
- TOP MOUNT BRACKETS: still clean engineered geometry — this is where wild follows
  the universal attachment grammar (organic finish stops at the mount nodes)
- No manufactured brand marks

KINETIC ELEMENT: Silver-shift #D8DDE6 on the tread contact surfaces and metal
structural housing frame. Impact Ring glyph on the outer face of one housing.
The tread track itself has a worn chrome-metal quality (use = polish).

RARITY FRAME (Common): Single #374350 border. No glow.

GREYSCALE TEST: At 64x64px reads as two wide tread units — the ground-contacting
lower elements. The width-at-bottom (mass-drop rule) is the defining characteristic.
The mount brackets at top are visible as the CHASSIS connection.

NEGATIVE: No bipedal leg form (these are treads, not limbs). No tall/narrow leg
silhouette. No upright stance elements (treads are low and wide). No Ironclad hex
bolts or Scrapjaw diagonal lines on the housing surfaces. No photoreal.
No painterly gradients. No rounded UI frame.
