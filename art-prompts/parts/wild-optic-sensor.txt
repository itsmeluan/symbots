Part icon: Optic Sensor (Wild, Common, HEAD slot, Kinetic).
Canvas 256x256 px. Front-facing or slight 3/4 view, part isolated on transparent background.

DESCRIPTION: "Scavenged lens array. One eye sees a little crooked."
A wild-origin HEAD unit — a sensor array that grew or was assembled from whatever
lens components were available in the field. One eye is slightly misaligned (personality:
"one eye sees a little crooked"), giving this HEAD an asymmetric, cobbled-together quality.
Still functions as a sensor crest — still has the mandatory horizontal sensor feature —
but the wild finish makes it feel like the bot grew this sensor cluster, not manufactured it.

HEAD SLOT SHAPE LANGUAGE (art-bible §3.3):
- MANDATORY: dominant horizontal sensor feature (visor / lens cluster / forward crest)
- Always narrower than CHASSIS shoulder width
- Must have a visual mass break from CHASSIS (neck-gap or narrowing below it)
- Must read as HEAD at 64x64px greyscale

WILD MANUFACTURER FINISH (art-bible §3.8):
- Organic/weathered contour on the primary sensor housing — curves that grew to their
  function, not machined geometry. The overall shape follows sensor function (smooth
  wide visor band) but the surface has weathered organic texture.
- Seams and attachment nodes are STILL ENGINEERED — the neck mount and chassis
  attachment point have standard mechanical geometry (universal attachment grammar holds)
- Biome-adaptive surface: for this common wild part, a neutral wild finish —
  slight mineral deposit veining or organic patina on the lens housing
- No manufactured branding: no diagonal slash lines (Scrapjaw), no hex bolts (Ironclad),
  no cable routing decoration (Boltwell)

HORIZONTAL SENSOR FEATURE (mandatory): A horizontal lens cluster — one primary large
lens (the "good eye") and one smaller slightly-offset secondary lens (the "crooked eye").
The cluster reads as a horizontal band because both lenses sit on the same horizontal
plane, but the slight offset gives it personality. The lenses are organic-feeling
(rounded apertures with weathered iris rings, not precision-machined optics).

KINETIC ELEMENT: Silver-shift #D8DDE6 on lens aperture rings and neck mount. Impact
Ring glyph (concentric circles) on the primary lens face (a natural read — concentric
lens rings echo the Impact Ring glyph).

RARITY FRAME (Common): Single #374350 border. No glow.

GREYSCALE TEST: At 64x64px — horizontal dual-lens sensor feature reads as a HEAD unit.
The slight asymmetry is readable at this scale (one lens visibly off-center).

NEGATIVE: No Ironclad hex bolts. No Scrapjaw diagonal lines. No Boltwell cable routing.
No symmetrical perfect lens arrangement (one eye is slightly crooked). No photoreal.
No painterly gradients. No rounded UI frame.
