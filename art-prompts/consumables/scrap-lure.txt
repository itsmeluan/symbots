Consumable icon: Scrap Lure. Canvas 64x64 px. Front-facing centered icon.

A device or lure that actively attracts wild Symbots to the player's location —
used to farm encounters deliberately. The "scrap" suggests it emits a signal that
reads as salvageable materials to wild machines. The "lure" is active attraction.

VISUAL CONCEPT: A small emitter/bait device that broadcasts a scrap-signal — a
compact box or stake with loose scrap metal pieces attached as physical bait, plus
a small signal emitter that broadcasts on frequencies wild Symbots respond to.
Should read as: deliberately shabby (it is Common rarity), effective-but-simple.
Something a field mechanic would cobble together from scrap to attract more scrap.

ICON COMPOSITION (centered, 64x64):
- Primary: a compact stake or box device with scrap metal pieces attached or piled
  on/around it — the "bait." The scrap pieces are small irregular metal fragments.
- Emitter element: a simple antenna or transmitter on the device pointing upward,
  with small simple signal arcs (clean, short — simpler than the Salvage Beacon's
  arcs because this is Common quality)
- The combination of "scrap pile" and "emitter" is the key visual read

COLOR: Alloy Ochre #C49A35 and Harvest Crimson #B33020 on the scrap metal bait pieces
(rusty, worn, genuine-scrap feel). Slate Gunmetal #374350 on the device body.
Small Wilderness Amber #C4721A emitter glow hint (active signal) — matte and subtle.
This is Common tier — nothing fancy.

UI CHROME RULES: Chamfered 45-degree frame corners. Single #374350 border (Common).
No glow. Plain and simple.

VISUAL DISTINCTION FROM SIGNAL JAMMER AND SALVAGE BEACON: Scrap Lure has SCRAP
PIECES as physical bait (tangible metal fragments visible). The other beacon/jammer
devices are pure electronics. The physical bait element makes the Scrap Lure instantly
distinguishable.

PALETTE: Slate Gunmetal #374350 device body; Alloy Ochre #C49A35 + Harvest Crimson
#B33020 on scrap bait pieces (rusty/old metal); Wilderness Amber #C4721A on emitter
tip (subtle active signal).

GREYSCALE TEST: At 32x32px — the scrap pile element beside/on the device reads as
"bait." The antenna/emitter is secondary.

NEGATIVE: No precision device look (this is cheap, cobbled together). No Boltwell
instrument finish. No clean gold reward color (this is Common, not Rare). No Lightning
Fork or element glyphs. No photoreal. No painterly gradients. No rounded frame corners.
