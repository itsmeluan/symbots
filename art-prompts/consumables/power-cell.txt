Consumable icon: Power Cell. Canvas 64x64 px. Front-facing centered icon.

A disposable energy cell for field refueling. Restores bot Energy in combat.
Should read as: portable battery / single-use charge pack / energy refill.

VISUAL CONCEPT: A compact single-use battery or energy cell — cylindrical or
rectangular, clearly a "throw-away charge pack." This is different from the
boltwell_cell_mk1 PART (which is a permanent equipment slot) — this is a
disposable field item, smaller and cruder.

ICON COMPOSITION (centered, 64x64):
- Primary: a compact rectangular or slightly cylindrical battery pack — thicker
  and shorter than a canister, more "block" than "tube." Visible positive/negative
  terminal contacts at one end.
- Charge indicator: a horizontal charge level stripe or indicator window on the
  face showing it is fully charged (bright Info Blue #4090CC — the UI's energy
  color from battle.md, not Volt cyan). The charge indicator uses the game's
  existing semantic: blue = energy.
- Contact terminals at the top/end: small rounded terminals for connection (not
  the same as Boltwell locking rings — these are disposable snaps, simpler)

NOTE ON COLOR: Use Info Blue #4090CC for the charge indicator, NOT Volt cyan
#2FE8E8. Per art-bible §4.5, the Energy bar in battle uses Info Blue (hue 210°,
deliberately offset from Volt cyan hue 180°) to distinguish Energy from Volt element.
This consumable restores Energy, so it uses the Energy semantic color.

UI CHROME RULES: Chamfered 45-degree frame corners. Single #374350 border (Common).
No element glow.

PALETTE: Slate Gunmetal #374350 cell body; Info Blue #4090CC charge indicator;
Bone White #F2EDDF on terminal contacts and structural highlights.

GREYSCALE TEST: At 32x32px reads as a battery/cell shape with a charge indicator
strip. The terminal contacts at one end differentiate it from a canister.

NEGATIVE: No Lightning Fork glyph (not a Volt elemental item — it restores Energy
as a resource, not as element). Do NOT use Volt cyan #2FE8E8 (use Info Blue
#4090CC for the energy charge indicator). No photoreal. No painterly gradients.
No rounded frame corners.
