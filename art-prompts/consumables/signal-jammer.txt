Consumable icon: Signal Jammer. Canvas 64x64 px. Front-facing centered icon.

A device that disrupts enemy detection and encounter triggering for a period of
overworld movement. The "jammer" concept reads as: broadcast interference,
signal disruption, electronic countermeasure.

VISUAL CONCEPT: A compact electronic jamming device — a rectangular device with
a small antenna array and visible interference waves broadcasting outward. The
jamming signal reads as disruption: broken/fragmented signal lines rather than
clean arcs (contrast with the Salvage Beacon's clean arcs). The interference effect
makes it visually distinct from the Salvage Beacon.

ICON COMPOSITION (centered, 64x64):
- Primary: a compact rectangular device body (Boltwell instrument quality — this
  is a sophisticated countermeasure). Slightly wider/flatter than the Salvage Beacon.
- Antenna: one or two short antennas on top — rigid, straight, machined (not organic)
- Interference waves: broken/fragmented arc lines radiating from the device on both
  sides — these are NOT clean concentric arcs (like Salvage Beacon) but INTERRUPTED
  arcs with visible signal-break gaps. The visual vocabulary of "jamming" is that the
  signal is disrupted and incomplete.
- Optional: a small X-mark or disruption indicator on the device face (reading:
  "signals blocked")

COLOR: Circuit Teal #2B6E68 or a muted blue-grey for the interference waves
(signal/electronic feeling, but NOT Volt cyan which is elemental). The device body
is Slate Gunmetal #374350. The interrupted arcs carry the muted electronic teal
to read as "disrupted signal."

UI CHROME RULES: Chamfered 45-degree frame corners. Double border + diamond pip (Rare).

PALETTE: Slate Gunmetal #374350 device body; Circuit Teal #2B6E68 on interrupted
signal arcs; Bone White #F2EDDF on antenna and structural highlights.

VISUAL DISTINCTION FROM SALVAGE BEACON: The Salvage Beacon has a horn emitter
pointing UP with clean gold arcs = broadcasting outward. The Signal Jammer has
antennas pointing UP with BROKEN teal arcs = disrupting reception. Same category
(beacon/emitter device) but visually opposite in arc quality and color.

GREYSCALE TEST: At 32x32px — the broken/interrupted arc pattern distinguishes this
from the Salvage Beacon's clean arcs even without color.

NEGATIVE: No clean unbroken signal arcs (jammer = disrupted arcs). No Lightning Fork.
No Volt cyan (use Circuit Teal). No weapon silhouette. No photoreal. No painterly
gradients. No rounded frame corners.
