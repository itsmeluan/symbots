Enemy Symbot: Arc Drone. Canvas 256x256 px. 3/4 side-view combat perspective.

CONCEPT: "A fast Boltwell interceptor. Dives in, discharges, and peels away before you
can answer." Aggressive airborne/hover unit — all speed and discharge, no staying power.
The visual must communicate: agile, dangerous in close, gone before you react.

SILHOUETTE DESCRIPTION:
Lightweight hover body plan — the LEGS are replaced by or transformed into hover/thruster
geometry (not ground-contact legs in the traditional sense — thruster pods or vectored
nozzles at the bottom instead of planted stance legs). CHASSIS is narrow and swept-back,
aerodynamic taper — minimal mass for speed. The dominant WEAPON element is the arc
emitter: a prominent forward-facing discharge array that points directly at the target
(per art-bible: ranged WEAPON angles forward-horizontal — this is the "pointing" element
that communicates threat direction). ARMS are narrow or folded, flush with CHASSIS for
aerodynamics. HEAD sensor is a forward-angled narrow visor — no wide scan array (it's
looking at one thing: the target).

BOLTWELL MANUFACTURER FINISH: Structural beams and struts visible on the narrow CHASSIS,
but lighter and more delicate than the Volt Sentinel's — this is a fast instrument, not
a fortified post. Cable bundles run from CHASSIS to the arc emitter. Exposed connector
rings where wings or thrust pods attach. Locking rings on emitter mount. The Boltwell
"high-tech instrument" finish reads here as cutting-edge prototype interceptor.

VOLT ELEMENT ACCENT: Cyan #2FE8E8 dominant on the arc emitter/WEAPON (the brightest
element on the drone). Lightning Fork glyph on the emitter face. Cyan energy trails on
thruster pods (suggesting speed). Structural cable conduits in cyan-tinted accent.
Slightly stronger glow than the Volt Sentinel (same element but more aggressive energy
— this thing is discharging constantly).

BREAK REGIONS (distinct targetable silhouette zones):
- CORE (region_fraction 28%): The CORE housing in the center of the narrow CHASSIS body
  — a reinforced structural node. Destroying it powers down the drone (reads as the
  "kill shot" on a lean body that has little else to hit).
- WEAPON (region_fraction 35%): The arc emitter — the prominent forward-pointing
  discharge array. It is the most visually distinctive element (it's why the drone
  is dangerous). Breaking it removes the forward-pointing shape, changing the silhouette
  significantly.

HOVER/FLIGHT VISUAL: Show the drone slightly above the ground baseline with thruster
pods angled downward or vectored. A faint energy wash or dust disturbance below the
thrusters (environment disturbance, not a glowing floor effect). The pose conveys
mid-dive — slightly pitched forward, emitter leading.

IDLE STANCE: Pitched forward in attack angle, emitter aimed at target, thrusters
compensating. Reads: "mid-approach." Dynamic, not hovering neutrally.

WORLD PALETTE BASE: Slate Gunmetal #374350 structural frame; Circuit Teal #2B6E68
on cabling; Bone White on structural nodes. Cyan #2FE8E8 dominant on emitter and thrust glow.

GREYSCALE TEST: At 64x64 greyscale — WEAPON emitter (forward-pointing), CHASSIS body
(narrow swept silhouette), and thruster LEGS (bottom pods) must read as three distinct
zones. The drone must not look like a ground unit.

NEGATIVE: No ground-planted stance (this is a hover/flying unit). No heavy armored
look (it's fast and light). No glow sphere for CORE. No facial expressions. No photoreal.
No painterly gradients.
