Enemy Symbot: Husk Walker. Canvas 256x256 px. 3/4 side-view combat perspective.

CONCEPT: "A gutted chassis lurching on borrowed power. No core spark, no allegiance —
it just walks." This is a derelict bot still moving on residual power — no active CORE,
no faction, just an empty shell that hasn't stopped yet. The visual must communicate
"empty, purposeless movement."

SILHOUETTE DESCRIPTION:
Large, slow-moving bipedal body plan. Heavy mass center — wide CHASSIS with visible
internal damage (empty CORE cavity visible as a hole or darkened interior, implying the
CORE is gone or dormant). LEGS are the dominant lower element: wide, planted, slow-walker
stance (NOT digitigrade — flat-footed heavy tread stance). HEAD sensor crest is
damaged/partial — the horizontal sensor feature should look cracked, dim, or half-missing,
but still structurally elevated (it defines the upper zone even when broken-looking).
The overall silhouette reads: "bigger than it should be, moving slower than it should,
nothing is home inside."

WILD/DERELICT FINISH: No manufacturer surface vocabulary — this is a husk. Surfaces are
weathered, dented, with stress fractures across panel lines. Some panels missing or bent
outward. No clean seams — the attachment grammar holds (it still equips parts) but looks
strained. Organic weathering: rust-patina, mineral deposits, vegetation threading through
panel gaps. No faction color, no deliberate paint scheme. Just aging metal.

ELEMENT TREATMENT: No strong element accent (core_element = 0/neutral). Use desaturated
Slate Gunmetal #374350 base with Alloy Ochre #C49A35 rust staining. Bone White on exposed
structural frame. No bright elemental glow. The visual palette should be the most muted
of the enemy roster — this thing is barely alive.

BREAK REGIONS (distinct targetable silhouette zones):
- LEG (region_fraction 25%): the wide-planted legs must each read as a distinct lower
  element. Breaking them collapses the bot's stance.
- HEAD (region_fraction 18%): even damaged, the elevated sensor region must be a
  readable upper zone. Breaking it removes the topmost element.

IDLE STANCE: Slow, slightly hunched, legs planted wide, arms hanging loosely at sides
(not raised for combat — this thing isn't planning, it's just walking). HEAD tilted
vaguely forward but without clear directional attention. The stance communicates "on
autopilot."

GREYSCALE TEST: At 64x64 greyscale — HEAD (elevated damaged sensor), wide planted LEGS,
and large CHASSIS cavity must read as three distinct zones.

NEGATIVE: No active glowing CORE visible (it's empty). No faction markings. No clean
surface finish. No photoreal. No painterly gradients. No facial expressions. Must NOT
look like a skeleton — it's a machine, not organic. Must NOT look purposeful or heroic.
