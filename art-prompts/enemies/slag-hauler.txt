Enemy Symbot: Slag Hauler. Canvas 256x256 px. 3/4 side-view combat perspective.

CONCEPT: "A hulking Ironclad transport turned brawler. It hurls molten slag and shrugs
off the return fire." Originally a heavy industrial transport, repurposed as a combat
unit. Bigger and more brutish than the Ironclad Sentry — less refined, more
workhorse-made-weapon. The visual communicates: industrial mass, incidental menace,
molten payload.

SILHOUETTE DESCRIPTION:
The largest non-boss enemy silhouette. Heavier mass than the Ironclad Sentry but with
more industrial/cargo-hauler origins (wider CHASSIS, lower center of gravity, transport
proportions). CHASSIS dominates — wider even than the Sentry, lower-slung, like a heavy
vehicle's cargo bay converted to armor. The WEAPON is a dominant ranged hurling element:
a large scoop or catapult-arm design at the front (ranged WEAPON = forward-horizontal angle
per art-bible — but this one has industrial/catapult character, not a precision barrel).
A visible slag containment feature on the CHASSIS or WEAPON (a molten-metal hopper or
heated chamber that glows amber). LEGS are massive tread-style or multi-pad stance — not
legs so much as heavy-duty ground anchors (think: industrial crane base adapted for combat).
HEAD is small and functional compared to the massive body — a thick-shielded sensor housing.

IRONCLAD MANUFACTURER FINISH: Dense perpendicular grid panel-lines, hex bolts and rivets
even more prominent than the Sentry (this was a work machine first). Thick armor plates
with visible wear from industrial use — not battle damage, but hauling-and-loading wear.
Heat-stained panel surfaces from constant slag exposure. Seams around the slag chamber
are visibly heat-warped.

THERMAL ELEMENT ACCENT: Heavy amber #F0900A molten glow from the slag chamber/WEAPON
hopper — this is the strongest Thermal glow in the common enemy roster (it literally
carries slag). Flame Chevron glyph on the WEAPON or CHASSIS-front. Orange-amber molten
material visible inside the hopper/chamber. Heat shimmer around the WEAPON. The CHASSIS
surface around the slag containment is heat-stained orange-amber at the seams.

BREAK REGIONS (distinct targetable silhouette zones):
- CHASSIS (region_fraction 45%): the massive cargo-converted torso. The largest break
  target in the game. Must look like it houses something heavy — when cracked, the
  internal cargo shows. Panel seams on the CHASSIS must imply "this opens" even unbroken.
- WEAPON (region_fraction 30%): the slag hurling element — a catapult arm, scoop, or
  industrial ejector. Must extend forward-and-slightly-up (ranged with area quality per
  art-bible WEAPON angle). Breaking it removes the primary threat and changes the top-
  forward silhouette dramatically.

IDLE STANCE: Heavy, low, feet (treads/pads) planted wide. WEAPON charged and raised
slightly (ready to hurl). HEAD sensor tracking forward. The stance is industrial labor
made threatening — it's not posturing, it's just loaded and ready. Enormous bottom-heavy
mass distribution.

WORLD PALETTE BASE: Slate Gunmetal #374350 heavy armor chassis; Alloy Ochre #C49A35
on heat-stained and oxidized metal areas; heat-warped amber #F0900A near the slag
containment; Bone White on structural frame nodes at panel edges.

GREYSCALE TEST: At 64x64 greyscale — WEAPON (forward-raised hurling element), massive
CHASSIS (widest body element with implied internal cavity), and broad-stance LEGS/tread
must read as three distinct mass zones. This must be visibly larger than the Ironclad Sentry.

NEGATIVE: No sleek or precision look (this was a hauler, not a combat machine). No fast
silhouette. No exposed CORE sphere. No facial expressions. No photoreal. No painterly
gradients. Do NOT make it look the same as the Ironclad Sentry — it is bigger, lower,
and more industrial/workhorse. No rounded UI.
