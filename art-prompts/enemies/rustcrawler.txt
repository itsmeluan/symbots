Enemy Symbot: Rustcrawler. Canvas 256x256 px. 3/4 side-view combat perspective.

SILHOUETTE DESCRIPTION:
A junkyard scavenger bot assembled from welded scrap — the first enemy most players face.
Small, low-to-the-ground, skittering body plan. Think: crab or spider-like LEGS spread
wide and low (the LEGS dominate the stance zone, wide and multi-contact); compact CHASSIS
torso above (narrow-ish, hunched forward, aggressive forward-cant); a recognizable ARM
region extending to one side with a claw-or-hook shape (the breakable ARM region must
read as a distinct silhouette element the player can aim at); a small HEAD sensor on top
with a horizontal visor-band or single forward-optic lens cluster. The bot looks SMALL
and FAST — low center of mass, many contact points.

SCRAPJAW MANUFACTURER FINISH: Asymmetric panel edges, diagonal ~45-degree slash panel
lines across the CHASSIS and ARM. Quick-release clasps and visible welded-over repairs.
Looks like it was bolted together from salvage, not manufactured cleanly. One ARM may be
larger than the other (personality signal — it grew by scavenging).

KINETIC ELEMENT ACCENT: Silver-shift metallic surfaces on moving parts and the claw edges.
Impact Ring glyph (concentric circles) subtly present on the ARM/claw region. Polished
chrome finish on the claw surface (the one allowed specular in Kinetic element).

BREAK REGIONS (must read as distinct targetable zones from silhouette):
- ARM (region_fraction 35%): the claw-arm must extend clearly from CHASSIS, visually
  separable at the joint seam. Breaking it changes the bot's outline meaningfully.
- HEAD (region_fraction 22%): the sensor crest is compact but elevated, with its
  horizontal visor feature. Breaking it removes the top element from the silhouette.

IDLE STANCE: Legs splayed, ARM raised in a ready-to-grab position, HEAD angled toward
target (sensor forward). Aggressive-but-small energy. Reads like a thing that lurks.

WORLD PALETTE BASE: Slate Gunmetal #374350 base chassis, rust-brown aged metal accents
(draw from Alloy Ochre #C49A35 and Harvest Crimson #B33020 as weathering), Bone White
#F2EDDF on exposed frame/structural highlights.

GREYSCALE TEST: At 64x64 greyscale — ARM region (claw), HEAD region (sensor crest), and
LEGS (spread stance foundation) must each be identifiable as separate zones by position.

NEGATIVE: No rounded UI corners. No clean factory finish (this is scrap/salvage). No
symmetrical arms (scrapjaw personality allows asymmetry). No visible CORE sphere.
No facial expressions. No human features. No photoreal. No painterly gradients.
