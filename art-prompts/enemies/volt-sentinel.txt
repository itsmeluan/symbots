Enemy Symbot: Volt Sentinel. Canvas 256x256 px. 3/4 side-view combat perspective.

CONCEPT: "A Boltwell guard unit. Patient, well-shielded, and quick to punish an opening."
This is a defensive, measured machine — not aggressive, but dangerous when provoked. High
structure and resistance, energy attacker. The visual should communicate: fortified,
watching, calculating.

SILHOUETTE DESCRIPTION:
Upright, symmetrical, guard-post stance. Heavy CHASSIS torso — broad, tall, the dominant
shape with shield-like planar surfaces that imply armor layering. LEGS are planted,
wide-based, immovable (defense role — broad, squat, multiple ground contacts). ARMS are
flush with CHASSIS, not extended aggressively — they carry capacitor/emitter geometry
rather than melee reach (energy attacker, not physical). HEAD sensor crest is the most
technically elaborate element: elevated, with a prominent horizontal scan-array (multiple
sensor nodes across a horizontal visor band) — tactical, all-seeing quality. The
"punishment for an opening" read comes from subtle energy-emitter geometry on ARMS or
CHASSIS that implies ranged discharge capability.

BOLTWELL MANUFACTURER FINISH: Exposed structural beams visible on CHASSIS panels —
deliberate voids and struts between solid nodes. Cable routing visible on exterior,
locking rings where major structural members join. Conduit bundles at CHASSIS-ARM and
CHASSIS-HEAD seams. All voids between struts are filled with darkened space (not missing
panels — designed cutouts). High-tech instrument quality. Looks purpose-built, not salvaged.

VOLT ELEMENT ACCENT: Cyan #2FE8E8 on energy emitter geometry (ARM capacitor nodes or
CHASSIS-mounted emitter plates), conducting cables/conduits in the Boltwell structural
routing, HEAD sensor glow. Lightning Fork glyph (bifurcated diagonal bolt) on the
emitter face or CHASSIS panel. Subtle cyan ambient glow on the emitter nodes (~25%
opacity, reinforcing the Rare or lower rarity this enemy carries).

BREAK REGIONS (distinct targetable silhouette zones):
- HEAD (region_fraction 24%): the elevated scan-array HEAD must be clearly distinct —
  elevated above CHASSIS, wider than expected (horizontal sensor bank), separated by
  a neck narrowing. Breaking it removes the distinctive scan array.
- CORE (region_fraction 30%): a visible "core housing" panel in the center CHASSIS —
  a heavier structural node that implies the CORE location (Boltwell exposes structure,
  so the core housing reads as a prominent reinforced panel in the chest). Breaking it
  cracks that panel open.

IDLE STANCE: Upright, feet planted, arms at sides or slightly angled outward with
capacitor nodes facing forward. HEAD sensor scanning forward (horizontal visor active).
Reads: "on duty, waiting, measuring."

WORLD PALETTE BASE: Slate Gunmetal #374350 structural chassis; Circuit Teal #2B6E68
on exposed conduit/cable runs; Bone White #F2EDDF on load-bearing structural nodes.
Cyan #2FE8E8 accents on emitter nodes and sensor glow.

GREYSCALE TEST: At 64x64 greyscale — elevated HEAD (scan array), wide planted LEGS,
and CHASSIS with visible structural NODE at center (core housing) must read as distinct zones.

NEGATIVE: No Scrapjaw salvage look (this is clean Boltwell precision). No aggressive
forward lean (guard stance = upright). No exposed glow sphere for CORE. No facial
expressions. No photoreal. No painterly gradients. No rounded UI.
