Enemy Symbot: Ironclad Sentry. Canvas 256x256 px. 3/4 side-view combat perspective.

CONCEPT: "An Ironclad line-holder. Slow, overbuilt, and radiating heat you have to crack
through." The tank archetype. This machine was made to stand its ground and absorb damage.
The visual must communicate: immovable, heavily armored, hot.

SILHOUETTE DESCRIPTION:
Maximum defense mass distribution. The CHASSIS is the widest, most dominant element —
thick convex slab-armored torso with chamfered corners, the defining "wall" shape of a
tank build (per art-bible §3.3: defense CHASSIS = wide, convex, blocklike). LEGS are
wide and squat — many ground contacts, planted firmly, heavy-duty stance (art-bible:
defense LEGS = wide, planted, squat, multiple ground contacts). ARMS are broad and blocky,
not extended aggressively — they sit flush with the massive CHASSIS, reinforcing the "wall"
silhouette rather than extending it. HEAD is compact but still distinctly elevated — a
hardened sensor housing with a narrow visor slit (armored HEAD reads narrower than
normal because the priority is CHASSIS mass). The overall silhouette reads: widest at
CHASSIS, LEGS spread wide below, HEAD small and hardened above. Unmistakably a tank.

IRONCLAD MANUFACTURER FINISH: Dense perpendicular grid panel-lines across every surface —
the heavy-industrial, armored-plate aesthetic. Prominent hex bolts and rivets on every
panel junction (readable even at icon scale). Planar, faceted surfaces — no organic
curves, just flat plane faces joined at hard angles. Thick-plate register: each armor
panel looks like it could stop a vehicle. The surface reads: "overbuilt on purpose."
Heat vents are visible on the CHASSIS sides — slotted geometric vents with amber glow
inside (the enemy uses heat_vent skill, so visual heat-venting geometry is accurate).

THERMAL ELEMENT ACCENT: Amber #F0900A glow emanating from heat vents in the CHASSIS
sides and from panel seams where heat bleeds through. Flame Chevron glyph (upward-stacked
V shapes) on the CHASSIS chest plate or heat vent surround. Subtle amber emissive bloom
around the vent areas (Thermal always gets emissive bloom). The bot radiates heat —
conveys danger through temperature, not aggressive posture.

BREAK REGIONS (distinct targetable silhouette zones):
- CHASSIS (region_fraction 40%): The massive armored torso IS the primary break target.
  The CHASSIS must look like it CAN be cracked — despite being the biggest element, it
  needs visible panel seams, vent gaps, and a structural hierarchy that suggests "this
  thick armor has a weak point." When broken, the CHASSIS panel splits open, revealing
  the interior.
- ARM (region_fraction 30%): The broad blocky arms are secondary targets. Each ARM
  must read as a distinct element against the CHASSIS despite both being wide/blocky —
  differentiate with arm joint seam and different panel-line direction.

IDLE STANCE: Upright, immovable. Arms at sides, slightly raised in a blocking position.
Legs planted. HEAD visor scanning forward. Reads: "this is where I stand. Come and try."
Zero forward lean — it is the obstacle, the player must come to it.

WORLD PALETTE BASE: Slate Gunmetal #374350 dominant armor surface; Alloy Ochre #C49A35
on oxidized/heated metal areas; Bone White on structural frame highlights where visible
at seams. Amber #F0900A on heat vents and Thermal glyphs.

GREYSCALE TEST: At 64x64 greyscale — wide planted LEGS (foundation), massive CHASSIS
(the body — widest element), and compact HEAD (elevated, narrow) must read as three
distinct mass zones. ARM must be identifiable at the CHASSIS edge.

NEGATIVE: No Scrapjaw asymmetry or salvage quality (Ironclad is deliberately built).
No aggressive forward lean (tank stance = immovable upright). No exposed CORE sphere.
No facial expressions. No fast/agile posture. No photoreal. No painterly gradients.
No rounded UI.
