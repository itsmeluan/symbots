UI element: Dark/Inset Panel Frame. Canvas 256x256 px. 9-slice sub-panel background.

A recessed variant of the standard panel — used for sub-panels that sit INSIDE the
standard panel frame. For example: the stat value display area within an inventory card,
or the active move detail area within the move panel. The dark panel reads as "sunken
into" the parent panel surface.

ANATOMY:
1. OUTER EDGE: 1px C-1 HUD Dark #1E2229 — this is the recessed "shadow" edge
   (darker than C-5, implying the sub-panel is pressed into the surface)
2. CHAMFERED CORNERS: 45-degree cuts, ~10px diagonal (slightly tighter than the
   standard panel — it is a sub-panel, proportionally smaller)
3. BODY FILL: C-1 HUD Dark #1E2229 — the darkest UI surface color
   This reads as the deepest/most recessed surface layer
4. INNER BORDER: 1px C-5 Divider #4B5668 at 1px inset from the outer edge —
   provides separation between the dark fill and the parent C-2 surface below it
   (when this sub-panel sits on a standard panel, the C-5 inner border lifts it
   slightly from the C-1 base)
5. No top accent highlight (this surface is recessed — light doesn't catch it)

9-SLICE CENTER: Flat C-1 #1E2229 fill, zero decoration.

VISUAL RELATIONSHIP TO STANDARD PANEL:
Standard panel (C-2 #2C3340 fill) > Dark panel sub-area (C-1 #1E2229 fill).
The three-layer depth hierarchy is: C-1 (deepest) → C-2 (mid) → C-3 (interactive surface).
This dark panel occupies the C-1 layer, reading as most recessed.

USES: Value display wells, active-item slots in inventory when viewing detail,
the core XP bar background, any inset display area.

GREYSCALE TEST: Visibly darker than the standard panel fill. The inner C-5 border
lifts it from the absolute darkness (provides a 1px brighter edge that frames the void).

NEGATIVE: NO ROUNDED CORNERS. No gradient. No glow. No organic curves. No tiling
decoration in stretch zone. No photoreal.
