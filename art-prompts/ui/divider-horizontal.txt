UI element: Horizontal Divider Rule. Canvas 256x4 px. 9-slice (horizontal stretch).

A thin horizontal rule used to visually separate sections within a panel —
between stat groups in the Workshop, between log entries, between menu items.
Reads as a precise engineered line, not a decorative embellishment.

ANATOMY (256x4 px, top-to-bottom):
- Row 1 (1px): C-1 HUD Dark #1E2229 — shadow above the rule
- Row 2 (2px): C-5 Divider #4B5668 — the rule itself (the visible line)
- Row 3 (1px): C-2 Mid Panel #2C3340 — subtle highlight below, lifts the rule
  from the background (implies a machined groove carved into the panel surface)

The 4px total height gives the rule a slight engraved quality — not just a 1px line
but a narrow groove with shadow above and highlight below.

END CAPS (left 8px / right 8px):
The end caps may taper slightly — the outermost 1px at each end can fade to C-1
(as if the groove meets the panel wall), giving a natural terminus. Or simply
terminate cleanly at the 8px margin — both are acceptable.

STRETCH ZONE (center 240px+):
The 3-row stripe (C-1 / C-5 / C-2) tiles horizontally without any modification.
Flat stripes — no decoration, no ticks, no end ornaments in the stretch zone.

GREYSCALE TEST: At 4px height the three distinct brightness rows (dark / mid / light)
read as an engraved line in any surface.

NEGATIVE: No organic curves. No rounded end caps. No gradient. No glow. No decorative
ticks or marks in the center zone. No photoreal. Keep it minimal — this is a separator,
not a feature.
