HUD element: General Panel Frame / Combat Log Frame. Canvas 256x128 px.
9-slice panel (border only — center transparent). Flat UI.

A reusable chamfered-corner panel frame for HUD and UI surfaces. Per art-bible §3.6
and §7.1: "Panel shapes: straight lines, chamfered corners (45° cuts, not rounded),
hard right angles. No flowing curves." The chamfer IS the UI's signature shape.

ANATOMY:
This sprite is a FRAME ONLY — the center (256-24)x(128-24) = 232x104 px area is
fully transparent (alpha = 0). The frame border is 12px wide on all sides.

FRAME STRUCTURE:
- Outer edge: 1-2px C-5 Divider/Chamfer line #4B5668
- Frame body (the 12px border zone): C-1 HUD Dark #1E2229 base plate
- Inner edge: 1px C-5 Divider line #4B5668
- Chamfered corners: at each corner, the outer edge makes a 45-degree cut
  approximately 10-12px long (matching the 12px margin so the chamfer fits cleanly)
- The four corner chamfers are the most visually distinctive element —
  they are the UI's signature

VARIANTS (generate 2 at 256x128):

VARIANT 1 — STANDARD PANEL (general use):
- Frame: C-1 #1E2229 body, C-5 #4B5668 outline (as above)
- No inner glow, no accent color
- Alpha center

VARIANT 2 — ACTIVE/SELECTED PANEL (when a panel is focused/selected in combat):
- Same frame geometry
- Inner edge line: Info Blue #4090CC instead of C-5 (selection highlight)
- A subtle inner shadow/glow at 4px inset from the inner edge in Info Blue #4090CC
  at low opacity (~30%)
- Alpha center

COMBAT LOG FADING FRAME NOTE: Per hud.md, the combat log uses "transparent corner
text where each new line fades in." The fading-transparency treatment is handled
by the UI programmer (alpha animation on the TextureRect/Label nodes). This panel
frame sprite provides the base chrome; the transparency is applied at runtime.

9-SLICE DELIVERY:
The 12px margins must be clean and consistent so the 9-slicer can tile the edge
bands without visual artifacts. The CENTER is 100% transparent (alpha channel = 0).

GREYSCALE TEST: The frame reads as a rectangular border with four distinctive
chamfered corners. The chamfer angles are visible at small scale.

NEGATIVE: NO ROUNDED CORNERS. The chamfer is the game's UI signature. No gradient
fills in the frame body. No organic curves. No decorative detail in the stretch bands
(only the corners have unique geometry). No glow unrelated to selection state.
No photoreal. No painterly gradients.
