HUD element: Heat Gauge. Canvas 256x32 px horizontal (primary). Flat UI, 9-slice.

The Heat gauge tracks bot internal heat buildup during combat. High heat risks
overheat. This is the game's third resource bar and the most accessibility-critical
because it spans amber-to-red, a known Deuteranopia risk. Per battle-visual-design.md
§2.3, three distinct zone colors with MANDATORY non-color channels for each zone.

THREE HEAT ZONES (generate each as a variant):

ZONE 1 — COOL (0-69%):
- Fill: C-3 Chrome Interactive #3A4455 (blue-slate — cool temperature read)
- No warning indicator on the bar itself
- Non-color channel: numeric readout (placed by UI programmer, not baked in)
- Reads as: "under control, no action needed"

ZONE 2 — WARNING / AMBER (70-89%):
- Fill: Thermal Amber #F0900A
- A small ⚠ marker appears at the 70% fill threshold (baked into the bar sprite
  as a ZONE MARKER LINE at the 70% position — a thin C-6 #E8E8E8 vertical tick
  at 70% of the inner fill area, so the threshold is always visually marked)
- Non-color channel: the ⚠ icon (placed as overlay by UI programmer) + numeric value
- Reads as: "attention required"

ZONE 3 — DANGER / OVERHEAT (90-100%):
- Fill: Danger Red #CC3020
- A second zone marker tick at 90% of the inner fill area
- Non-color channel: the ⚠ icon upgrades to a critical indicator (UI programmer)
  + numeric value
- Reads as: "imminent overheat"

SPRITE DELIVERY:
Generate 3 fill variants at 256x32:
1. Cool fill (#3A4455), with zone marker ticks at 70% and 90% positions as thin
   vertical C-6 lines (subtle — they mark thresholds, not dominate the design)
2. Warning fill (#F0900A), same zone markers
3. Danger fill (#CC3020), same zone markers

The container shape is identical to the Structure and Energy bars:
- Chamfered 45-degree corners (NOT rounded)
- C-2 #2C3340 empty track
- C-5 #4B5668 outline

ACCESSIBILITY NOTE: The three fill colors span a Deuteranopia risk range. The zone
marker ticks at 70% and 90% are the non-color channel for zone transitions — they
must be visible in greyscale. Do NOT rely on the color change alone to communicate
the zone shift.

9-SLICE MARGINS: Left 8px, Right 8px, Top 4px, Bottom 4px.

GREYSCALE TEST: The two zone marker tick lines at 70% and 90% of fill width must
be visible in greyscale. The three fill zones are NOT distinguishable in greyscale
— the ticks carry that information.

NEGATIVE: No Flame Chevron glyph (heat ≠ Thermal element — heat is an internal
resource, not an element state). No rounded corners. No organic curves. No decorative
tiling in stretch zone. No photoreal.
