Workshop background: Bench Backdrop. Canvas 1024x600 px.

The full-screen background for the Workshop / Oficina build screen. Per art-bible §2.6:
"Neutral-to-warm indoor workshop light from above and slightly front — an articulated
bench lamp. Even, high-ambient, low-shadow; every slot and attachment point clearly lit
with no boundary-obscuring shadow. Background darker than subject but controlled and
stable — no flicker, no atmospheric motion."

MOOD: The lowest-energy state in the game. "The workshop as a laboratory." Focused,
warm, exact, unhurried. The mechanic is at the bench — this is THEIR space.

COMPOSITION:
The backdrop depicts the workshop environment behind where the bot would be displayed.
The bot and its part slots are composited on TOP of this background — so this image
should contain NO robot sprites and NO slot frames (those are separate UI assets).

BACKGROUND ELEMENTS:
- WORKBENCH SURFACE: a sturdy industrial bench across the lower 30% of the image.
  Surface material: Slate Gunmetal #374350 with Alloy Ochre #C49A35 wear marks.
  The bench surface has faint panel-line geometry (Ironclad-adjacent machined surface).
  Some tools implied on the bench edge (silhouettes of a wrench, some bolts, a tray)
  but LOW-DETAIL and NOT the visual focus — they recede as background props.
- BENCH LAMP: an articulated lamp arm visible in the upper portion, its head aimed
  downward-center where the bot display area would be. The lamp is the warm light
  source. Warm amber-white glow from the lamp head (not Thermal element — workshop
  light is practical/physical). The lamp arm is Slate Gunmetal, functional-looking.
- TOOL STORAGE / SHELVING: implied shelving or pegboard in the upper-left or
  upper-right background areas — LOW-DETAIL silhouettes of organized tools,
  part bins, components. Reads as "an engineer's organized workspace" without
  any single item competing for attention.
- WALL SURFACE: the background wall above the bench. Darker than the bench surface
  (Slate Gunmetal #374350 to HUD Dark #1E2229 range). Some faint panel-line texture
  but matte and receding. Possibly a faint circuit-trace pattern (Circuit Teal #2B6E68
  at very low opacity) suggesting this is the Oficina of a field mechanic who lives
  in the mechanical wilderness.

LIGHTING TREATMENT (art-bible §2.6):
- WARM KEY: the bench lamp casts warm neutral-to-warm light on the center of the
  image (the bot-display zone). This is where the bot sprite will appear.
- HIGH AMBIENT FILL: the entire scene has a raised ambient light level — no deep
  dark shadows anywhere. The workshop is "fully lit, every corner seen."
- ZERO VIGNETTE: unlike the overworld or combat screens, the Workshop has NO
  atmospheric vignette at the edges. Every corner reads at near-equal ambient brightness.
- NO ATMOSPHERIC MOTION: this is a completely still backdrop. No flicker, no pulse,
  no ambient particle effects.

CENTER ZONE (the bot-display area):
The center of the image (roughly 400x400 px centered) should be the most evenly lit,
least-detailed zone — a clean lit surface where the modular bot will be composited
on top. Do not place any foreground objects in this zone.

PALETTE (art-bible §2.6 / §4.6 Workshop):
Slate Gunmetal #374350 dominant (bench, walls, tools); Alloy Ochre #C49A35 on
worn metal surfaces; Bone White #F2EDDF on structural highlights at bench edges;
Circuit Teal #2B6E68 as very subtle background circuit traces; Wilderness Amber
#C4721A on the lamp warm-light area only (matte, NO Flame Chevron).

GREYSCALE TEST: The scene reads as a well-lit interior workshop with a bench,
lamp, and tool storage. Depth is implied by relative brightness (bench = brighter,
wall = darker) not by contrast or dramatic shadow.

NEGATIVE: No bot sprites. No slot frames. No UI chrome. No HUD elements.
No dark atmospheric vignette at edges. No flickering light. No dramatic shadows.
No Flame Chevron glyphs on ANY surface (the warm amber lamp light is physical light,
not a Thermal element marker). No outdoor environment elements. No photoreal. No PBR.
