Workshop: Empty Equip Slot Frame. Canvas 80x80 px. 9-slice frame, transparent center.

The equip slot chrome in the Workshop screen. There are 8 equip slots (one per slot
type: CORE, CHASSIS, CHIPSET, ENERGY_CELL, HEAD, ARMS, LEGS, WEAPON). This frame
is the EMPTY state — no part is equipped in this slot. The part icon renders inside
the 64x64 transparent center when a part is equipped.

Per art-bible §2.6: Workshop interactive elements stay chamfered even if the ambient
Workshop framing can be slightly softer. The slot frames ARE interactive (the player
taps to equip/unequip) so they use strict chamfered geometry.

FRAME ANATOMY:
- OUTER: 80x80 px
- CENTER TRANSPARENT: 64x64 px (alpha = 0) — the part icon renders here
- FRAME BORDER: 8px on all sides
- OUTER EDGE: 2px C-5 Divider #4B5668
- FRAME BODY (the 8px zone): C-2 Mid Panel #2C3340
- INNER EDGE: 1px C-5 #4B5668
- CHAMFERED CORNERS: 45-degree cuts at all four corners, ~8px diagonal (fits
  within the 8px margin without overlapping the transparent center)

EMPTY-STATE INDICATOR:
When empty, the 64x64 transparent center zone shows the bench backdrop through it,
but the game engine typically places a SLOT GLYPH ICON (from the icons/ set) in the
center at ~50% opacity to indicate which slot type this frame represents. Do NOT
bake any slot glyph into this sprite — the glyph is overlaid by the UI programmer.

SUBTLE EMPTY INDICATOR: A faint dotted or dashed inner border at the edge of the
transparent zone (1px, C-5 at ~40% opacity) — a very subtle "empty" visual cue that
reads as "this slot awaits a part." Optional: a very faint X or + mark at the center
of the transparent zone at ~20% opacity. Keep this extremely subtle — the slot glyph
icon placed by the UI programmer carries the primary "empty slot" read.

GREYSCALE TEST: The frame reads as a chamfered-corner square border. The center
transparent zone is clearly distinct from the border zone by contrast.

NEGATIVE: NO ROUNDED CORNERS. No part icon baked in. No slot glyph baked in.
No organic curves. No glow in empty state. No photoreal.
