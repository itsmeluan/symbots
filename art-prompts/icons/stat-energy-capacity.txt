Icon: ENERGY CAPACITY stat glyph. Canvas 32x32 px. Monochrome, white on transparent.

Energy Capacity is the maximum Energy the bot can hold (the "pool size"). Higher
capacity = more moves before running empty. The icon must communicate:
storage capacity, a reservoir that holds charge, the SIZE of the energy pool.

ICON DESIGN: A large/full battery cell motif:
- A tall rectangular battery/cell outline — taller and more prominent than the
  ENERGY_CELL SLOT glyph, to communicate "maximum capacity / full pool."
- Terminal contacts at the top: the standard positive/negative terminal tabs
  protruding above the cell body (same as slot-energy-cell.txt).
- FULL CHARGE INDICATOR: the interior of the cell is shown as MOSTLY FILLED
  with horizontal fill lines or a fill block — approximately 80-90% filled
  (communicating "capacity" / "how full this can be," not current charge).
  The fill is represented by 3-4 horizontal lines inside the cell body, or
  a solid inner fill block leaving only a small empty space at the top.
- 2px cell outline, 1px fill lines or fill block outline.

DISTINCTION FROM STAT-RECHARGE: Energy Capacity = the SIZE of the pool (full
or large cell). Recharge = the FLOW into the pool (cell with an upward arrow).
Capacity emphasizes the container; Recharge emphasizes the replenishment rate.

DISTINCTION FROM SLOT-ENERGY-CELL: The slot glyph is the same battery form
but with only a charge stripe (not a near-full fill), used to identify the slot.
The capacity stat icon shows a near-full fill state to communicate "maximum size."

NEGATIVE: No upward arrow (that is Recharge). No circuit traces (that is
Processing/Chipset). No color. No element glyph. No organic shapes. No photoreal.
