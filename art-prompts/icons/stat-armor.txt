Icon: ARMOR stat glyph. Canvas 32x32 px. Monochrome, white on transparent.

Armor reduces incoming physical damage. The icon must communicate: damage
mitigation, physical protection, deflection of force.

ICON DESIGN: A shield / layered plate motif:
- A classic shield outline — but angular and chamfered (not a medieval heraldic
  shield with rounded curves). Think: a geometric kite-shield or hexagonal shield
  form with hard flat edges and a slight taper to a bottom point.
- Chamfered top corners (45-degree cuts at the upper-left and upper-right of
  the shield — consistent with the game's machined-geometry visual language).
- A horizontal band across the upper third of the shield interior (a reinforcement
  bar — the Ironclad heavy-plate aesthetic applied to a shield form).
- 2px outline, 1px interior band line. Bold and compact.

DISTINCTION FROM STRUCTURE: Structure is a flat plate (horizontal panel).
Armor is a shield (angled, protective, slightly taller than wide). The taper to
a bottom point is the key shape differentiator.

NEGATIVE: No curved organic shield shape (angular/chamfered). No color.
No element glyph. No sword or weapon overlaid on the shield. No photoreal.
