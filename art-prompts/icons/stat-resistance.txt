Icon: RESISTANCE stat glyph. Canvas 32x32 px. Monochrome, white on transparent.

Resistance reduces incoming energy/elemental damage. The icon must communicate:
deflection of energy, element repulsion, a barrier against elemental force.

ICON DESIGN: A deflection chevron / energy-ward motif:
- Two or three parallel angular chevrons (V-shapes pointing LEFT — the direction
  of deflection, as if energy approaches from the right and is deflected away to
  the left by the chevron geometry).
- The chevrons are bold, angled at approximately 60 degrees. They stack vertically
  with equal spacing (like multiple wave-deflection bars).
- The leftmost/outermost chevron is the largest; inner chevrons are progressively
  smaller (or all the same size — both work; equal size is cleaner at 32x32).
- 2px stroke, hard angular corners (no rounded bends in the chevron angles).

DISTINCTION FROM ARMOR: Armor is a shield (static protective form).
Resistance is deflection chevrons (dynamic, directional — energy bouncing off).
Armor = physical; Resistance = elemental. The directionality of the chevrons
communicates the "deflect" action vs. the shield's "absorb" stance.

NOTE: The Flame Chevron (Thermal element glyph) points UPWARD.
Resistance chevrons point LEFT. Different direction, different context.
The art-bible forbids confusing element glyphs with UI semantics — these must
be clearly distinct at 32x32.

NEGATIVE: No upward-pointing chevrons (that is the Flame Chevron / Thermal glyph).
No shield shape (that is Armor). No color. No element glyph. No photoreal.
