Icon: CORE slot glyph. Canvas 32x32 px. Monochrome, white on transparent.

The CORE slot icon for the Workshop slot frame. This is a SLOT MARKER GLYPH —
a monochrome outline that appears inside an empty Workshop slot frame to indicate
"this is where the CORE goes." It is NOT the rendered CORE sphere asset
(which is a Workshop/UI-only 256x256 colored 3D-feeling sphere).

Per art-bible §3.3 and §5.3: the CORE is the only fully-organic-contour object in
the game — a smooth sphere. The slot glyph reflects this unique identity by being the
only CIRCULAR glyph in the slot icon set. This is the explicit exception: circles are
forbidden in combat UI (break pips, reticles) but the CORE glyph is not a combat
control — it is a slot marker in the Workshop, where the circle communicates
"this is the one spherical, non-angular thing in the game."

ICON DESIGN: A sphere outline — a perfect circle:
- A clean circle outline, 2px stroke weight
- Centered within the 32x32 canvas with ~4px margin on all sides
- An inner design hint: two or three faint concentric interior lines OR a subtle
  cross/meridian line (like a globe's latitude/longitude lines) to make the circle
  read as a SPHERE rather than a flat disc or ring
- The meridian lines are 1px, subtler than the outer circle stroke
- The overall form is immediately "sphere" at 32x32 — simple and singular

IMPORTANT DESIGN NOTE: This is the ONLY circular slot glyph. All seven other slot
glyphs are angular/rectilinear. The CORE's circle is a deliberate exception that
communicates "this slot is different — the thing that goes here is unique."

NEGATIVE: No flat disc (it reads as sphere via meridian lines). No complex interior
detail. No color fills. No element glyph. No weapon or limb silhouette. No photoreal.
