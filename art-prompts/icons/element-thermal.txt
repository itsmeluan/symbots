Icon: THERMAL element glyph. Canvas 32x32 px. Colored icon on transparent background.

The canonical Thermal element glyph. Per art-bible §4.2: the Thermal glyph is the
Flame Chevron — "upward-stacked Vs."

GLYPH DESIGN — FLAME CHEVRON:
Two or three V-shapes stacked vertically, each pointing UPWARD, with the larger
V at the bottom and smaller Vs above — like a flame rising through upward chevrons.
The canonical shape:
- BOTTOM V: the widest chevron, opening at the base, tips pointing up (the largest)
- MIDDLE V: a narrower chevron above and centered on the bottom V, same upward orientation
- TOP V (optional): a small narrow chevron or single upward point at the top
- The stacked-V arrangement reads like a stylized flame or heat-rising pattern
- The chevrons are angled lines (not curved) — this is a mechanical/engineering glyph,
  not an organic flame shape. Hard straight edges, angular chevron geometry.
- Bold, 2-3px stroke weight, clean hard edges
- Centered within the 32x32 canvas

COLOR: Thermal Amber #F0900A on transparent background. Solid filled glyph.
Optional: a very subtle emissive bloom suggestion at the tip of the top chevron
(1-2px soft spread at ~50% opacity in the same amber) — Thermal always gets
a bloom per art-bible. Captured as a slight glow at the tip in this still icon.

VERIFIED CONTRAST ON C-1 #1E2229: Thermal amber ≈ 5.8:1 (per art-bible §4.5).

DISTINCTION FROM REWARD GOLD: Thermal Amber #F0900A is hue 36°; Reward Gold
#E8B820 is hue 50° + higher luminance. The Flame Chevron glyph shape ensures
Thermal reads as Thermal, not as a reward/rarity indicator (art-bible §4.7 risk pair 2).

GREYSCALE TEST: The upward-stacked V shapes read as a directional chevron pattern
in greyscale — pointing upward, like heat rising.

NEGATIVE: No organic flame shape (no curved fire visual — this is angular chevrons).
No Lightning Fork shape. No circle. No soft brush strokes. No rounded edges.
No Flame Chevron on any environment surface without an emissive bloom (the glyph
only appears on elemental content). No photoreal.
