Icon: KINETIC element glyph. Canvas 32x32 px. Colored icon on transparent background.

The canonical Kinetic element glyph. Per art-bible §4.2: the Kinetic glyph is the
Impact Ring — "concentric circles."

GLYPH DESIGN — IMPACT RING:
Two or three concentric circles, centered, like a ripple or shockwave from an impact.
The canonical shape:
- OUTER RING: the largest circle, 2-3px stroke, outermost border
- MIDDLE RING: a circle approximately 65% the diameter of the outer, same stroke
- INNER DOT/CIRCLE: a small solid circle or filled center dot at the exact center
  (the "impact point" — where the concentric rings originate from)
- The rings are PERFECT CIRCLES (or as close as pixel art allows at 32x32)
- All rings share the same center point exactly
- Each ring is the same stroke weight (2px)
- Gaps between rings are equal (the spacing reads as even ripple intervals)
- Centered within the 32x32 canvas with ~3px margin from the outer ring to the edge

COLOR: Kinetic Silver #D8DDE6 on transparent background. Solid filled rings
(not filled discs — the rings are outlines/circles, the spaces between are transparent).
The center dot is solid silver.

IMPORTANT — KINETIC VS STRUCTURAL WHITE (art-bible §4.2 note):
The Kinetic glyph renders on a C-1 dark plate (#1E2229) per the art-bible §4.2 rule:
"The Kinetic UI badge always renders on a fixed dark plate (#1E2229, target ≈7.5:1)
so it never floats on an ambiguous background." Design the glyph to read clearly on
that dark plate — silver on near-black is the render context.

DISTINCTION FROM UI ELEMENTS: Circles are FORBIDDEN as UI break pips (art-bible §3.6).
But the Kinetic Impact Ring is an ELEMENT GLYPH, not a UI pip — it lives on the element
badge (dark plate), not in the break pip row. It is the exception that proves the rule:
the glyph is round BECAUSE it communicates physical impact, never because it is a UI control.

GREYSCALE TEST: Concentric circles at 32x32 read as an impact/ripple/shockwave pattern.
The center dot anchors the "impact point" read.

NEGATIVE: No Lightning Fork shape. No Flame Chevron shape. No filled solid disc (rings only,
with transparent gaps between them except for the center dot). No square or angular
geometry on the rings themselves. No photoreal. No soft edges (crisp pixel circles).
