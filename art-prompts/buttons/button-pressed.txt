UI element: Generic Button — Pressed (active/depressed) state. Canvas 192x56 px. 9-slice.

The button's appearance at the moment the player taps/clicks and the action fires.
Per art-bible §7.4: "crisp, mechanical, snappy — chrome animates like a machine actuating
(quick ease-out, decisive settle)." The pressed state is the machine ACTUATING.

VISUAL CHANGE FROM NORMAL:
The button reads as physically depressed — pushed in. Two simultaneous changes:
1. The body DARKENS: drops to C-1 HUD Dark #1E2229 or slightly above it — the surface
   recedes as if compressed into the panel behind it.
2. The top highlight INVERTS: the 1px lighter line moves to the BOTTOM inner edge
   instead of the top (the classic pressed-button bevel inversion — light now catches
   the bottom because the surface is depressed).
3. Outer edge: C-5 Divider #4B5668 but slightly darker shade, or C-1 — pressed inward.
4. No glow of any kind in the pressed state (action is firing, not hovering).

ANATOMY:
1. OUTER EDGE: C-5 #4B5668 or near C-1 (slightly compressed look)
2. BUTTON BODY: C-1 HUD Dark #1E2229 — the darkest the button gets
3. BOTTOM HIGHLIGHT: 1px lighter line on BOTTOM inner edge (inverted from Normal)
4. TOP EDGE: no highlight (recessed)
5. CHAMFERED CORNERS: identical geometry — same 45-degree cuts, same margin

The pressed state is held for only 1-2 frames in the game (quick actuate, quick return).
The sprite captures the fully-pressed moment.

GREYSCALE TEST: Reads as darker than Normal state. The body fill distinction
(C-3 Normal vs C-1 Pressed) is a clear luminance step visible in greyscale.

9-SLICE: Same margins, featureless dark body in the stretch zone.

NEGATIVE: No glow. No Info Blue outline. No label text baked in. NO ROUNDED CORNERS.
No organic curves. No gradient. No photoreal.
