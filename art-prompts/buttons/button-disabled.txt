UI element: Generic Button — Disabled state. Canvas 192x56 px. 9-slice.

The button's appearance when the action is unavailable (not enough energy, wrong
context, gate not met). Must communicate "unavailable" without removing the button
from the layout — the player needs to know the action EXISTS but cannot be taken.

VISUAL CHANGE FROM NORMAL:
Full desaturation plus opacity drop. The button recedes visually without disappearing.

ANATOMY:
1. OUTER EDGE: C-5 Divider #4B5668 but at ~50% opacity (faded, not sharp)
2. BUTTON BODY: C-2 Mid Panel #2C3340 — same as empty bar track, implying "depleted"
3. NO top highlight (flat, no bevel — the button is inert)
4. CHAMFERED CORNERS: same geometry but edges feel less crisp (the fading handles this)
5. Overall alpha: ~60% opacity on the entire sprite (the UI programmer applies this
   in-engine, but the sprite itself should be drawn at the slightly-muted palette)

OPTIONAL INERT INDICATOR: A very subtle diagonal cross-hatch or slight noise texture
at low opacity over the body fill — communicates "inactive" as a non-color channel.
Keep it subtle (3-5% texture variation at most — the opacity drop is the primary signal).

NON-COLOR CHANNEL: The label text placed by the UI programmer will also be dimmed
(C-7 Secondary Text #98A4B4 instead of C-6 Primary #E8E8E8 on disabled buttons).
The sprite provides the dimmed background; the text color change is the co-channel.

GREYSCALE TEST: The disabled button is visibly lower luminance and lower contrast
than the Normal button, without disappearing. It recedes without vanishing.

9-SLICE: Same margins. Featureless muted fill in stretch zone.

NEGATIVE: No color highlight. No glow. No Info Blue (not interactive). No label text.
NO ROUNDED CORNERS. No organic curves. No photoreal.
