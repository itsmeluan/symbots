Overworld ground tile. Canvas 64x64 px. Top-down, seamlessly tileable.

This is one of FOUR terrain tiles that must be TELLABLE APART AT A GLANCE while all
four recede behind the character. In this game terrain is a gameplay lever, not
decoration: the terrain the player stands on decides which enemies they meet, so a
player must be able to see "I am in the grass, not the junkyard" without stopping to
look. Differentiate the four by HUE and TEXTURE REGISTER — never by contrast. Raising
contrast to stand out breaks the figure/ground contract for every tile in the set.

ZONE VOCABULARY (art-bible 6.5 — "Vegetation"): nature winning; machines being
absorbed back. Trailing overgrowth, root-cabling, reclaimed hulls.

CONTENT:
- PRIMARY: Ironmoss Green #3D7A4A — dominant, overgrown vegetation baseline
- SECONDARY (~18%): Circuit Teal #2B6E68 — exposed circuitry running like roots
  through the ground; the world is part machine
- TERTIARY (~8%): Alloy Ochre #C49A35 — dry earth showing through the cover, matte
- TEXTURE REGISTER: soft, layered, curved. Overlapping leaf-and-cable forms with no
  straight edges. This is the ORGANIC end of the terrain set — its contrast partner
  is the junkyard's angular wreckage.

CRITICAL — THE PREVIOUS DELIVERY FAILED ON THESE TWO. Read them twice:

1. FULLY OPAQUE. Every pixel at alpha 255. The last ground tile shipped at alpha
   70-120 with zero fully-opaque pixels, so it blended with the dark background and
   rendered as grey mud instead of green ground. No transparency anywhere, no soft
   alpha edges, no semi-transparent overlay layer.

2. LOW-FREQUENCY, LOW-CONTRAST. The last tile shipped as a high-contrast checkerboard
   of green/teal/ochre blocks, which read as visual noise competing with the
   character. Required instead: broad soft patches, gentle variation, the eye should
   slide over it. Keep the lightest and darkest pixel CLOSE in value. If the tile is
   interesting to look at on its own, it is wrong — it is a stage, not a subject
   (art-bible 3.5, 6.2).

SEAMLESS TILING: all four edges tile without a seam. The tile must hold up as a 40x26
grid without the repeat becoming visible — avoid any single distinctive element that
the eye can lock onto and count.

WORLD PALETTE ONLY: Ironmoss Green #3D7A4A, Circuit Teal #2B6E68, Alloy Ochre #C49A35.
No Harvest Crimson (reserved for hazard). No Bone White. No element colors on ground.

GREYSCALE TEST: reads as an even mid-tone surface with no element the eye catches.

NEGATIVE: No bot sprites. No UI chrome. No element glyphs on the ground. No glyphed
amber (art-bible 4.1 matte-amber discipline). No transparency. No high contrast. No
pixel-noise stippling. No photoreal. No PBR. No baked lighting or drop shadow. No
visible tile border.
