Overworld: Encounter Marker Frame. Canvas 96x96 px. Top-down map overlay.

A visual frame/ring rendered behind an enemy sprite marker on the overworld map
to indicate its presence zone and provide a consistent base for the enemy indicator.
The enemy sprite sits centered within this frame; the frame provides the
"this is an encounter point" visual signal without overwhelming the enemy sprite above it.

ANATOMY:
The frame is a subtle chamfered-rectangular ring (per UI shape grammar §3.6 —
hard edges, no organic curves). The center is transparent — the enemy sprite
renders on top of this frame.

RING GEOMETRY:
- Overall: 96x96 px outer bounds
- Ring border: 4px on all sides
- Chamfered corners: 45-degree cuts, ~10px diagonal
- Center: transparent (alpha = 0) — the enemy sprite goes here
- Effective inner transparent zone: approximately 80x80 px

STATES (generate 2 variants):

VARIANT 1 — IDLE/PATROL marker (enemy not yet aggroed):
- Ring fill: Harvest Crimson #B33020 at ~60% opacity — the environmental danger
  color from world palette (§4.1). This is the "enemy presence" signal in the world
  context (not UI red — this is §4.6 zone tinting, the world-surface hazard color).
- Ring outer edge: 1px C-5 Divider #4B5668 for separation from the background
- Optional: a very subtle slow pulse would be applied by the animator — the sprite
  captures the mid-cycle state (solid 60% opacity)

VARIANT 2 — ACTIVE/ENCOUNTER TRIGGERED (player is about to engage):
- Ring fill: Harvest Crimson #B33020 at ~90% opacity (more intense as trigger fires)
- Outer edge: Harvest Crimson at full opacity (the ring "lights up")
- Slight outer spread: 2-3px soft blur at 30% opacity beyond the ring

NON-COLOR CHANNEL: The ring shape (a rectangle of defined presence) communicates
"encounter zone" separate from color. In greyscale the ring reads as a border
around the transparent center where an enemy sprite would appear.

GREYSCALE TEST: Ring border (mid-tone) against transparent center and the map
background below — reads as a framing rectangle with chamfered corners.

NEGATIVE: No circular ring (this is a chamfered rectangular frame per UI shape
grammar). No rounded corners. No UI Dark background fill in the center (transparent).
No element glyphs on the frame. No photoreal. No organic curves on the frame itself.
