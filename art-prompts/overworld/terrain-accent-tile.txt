Overworld tile: Terrain Accent / Scrap Scatter. Canvas 64x64 px. Top-down.
Transparent background — overlaid on terrain-base-tile.png as a decoration layer.

This tile adds sparse environmental storytelling detail on top of the base ground.
Per art-bible §6.5: "Debris implies prior Symbots: a spent chassis or a broken part
half-buried in a zone tells the player 'others came before' — and rewards looking."
These accents are the game's narrative without text.

CONTENT: Small scattered debris elements — half-buried machine components, broken
panel fragments, mechanical detritus partially reclaimed by the environment.
The elements should be SPARSE (2-3 objects per tile maximum, with lots of empty
transparent area so the tile is used as scatter decoration, not wall-to-wall cover).

ELEMENT OPTIONS (choose 2-3 per tile, vary when generating multiple variants):
- A small broken panel fragment (Slate Gunmetal #374350 metal, worn edges)
- A partially-buried gear or joint component (Alloy Ochre #C49A35 oxidized)
- A short circuit-vine tendril (Circuit Teal #2B6E68) growing over a metal fragment
- A cracked energy cell casing (dark, inert — no glow; just the casing remains)
- Scattered small hex-head bolts or rivets (Bone White #F2EDDF structural details)

VISUAL RULES:
- Each element is LOW-FREQUENCY and LOW-CONTRAST against the base tile
- Elements are partial (half-buried, obscured) — not fully legible as specific parts.
  This communicates "remains of something" without being identifiable as a game item.
- No element should visually compete with a bot sprite placed nearby.
- Elements have organic weathering (vegetation growing over them, mineral deposits)
- NO GLOW, NO EMISSIVE effects on any debris element (they are inert/spent)

TRANSPARENCY: The tile has a transparent background — it composites over the base
tile. Only the debris elements are opaque. Sparse placement = mostly transparent.

GENERATE 3 VARIANTS for variety scatter:
- Variant A: more circuit-vine and organic overgrowth (Vegetation zone lean)
- Variant B: more angular panel fragments (Industrial-Debris zone lean)
- Variant C: mixed, neutral (general wilderness)

GREYSCALE TEST: In greyscale the debris elements read as slightly higher texture
than the base tile — visible but not dominant. They are decoration, not features.

NEGATIVE: No recognizable in-game part icons on the debris (these are ambient world
storytelling, not collectible indicators). No glowing elements. No enemy markers.
No UI chrome. No photoreal. No high-contrast foreground objects. No baked shadow.
