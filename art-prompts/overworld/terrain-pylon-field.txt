Overworld ground tile. Canvas 64x64 px. Top-down, seamlessly tileable.

This is one of FOUR terrain tiles that must be TELLABLE APART AT A GLANCE while all
four recede behind the character. In this game terrain is a gameplay lever, not
decoration: the terrain the player stands on decides which enemies they meet, so a
player must be able to see which terrain they are in without stopping to look.
Differentiate the four by HUE and TEXTURE REGISTER — never by contrast. Raising
contrast to stand out breaks the figure/ground contract for every tile in the set.

ZONE VOCABULARY (art-bible 6.5 — "Crystalline"): a place where energy crystallized.
Faceted mineral growth, sharp geodes, refractive veins. Volt-adjacent, ancient.

CONTENT:
- PRIMARY: Circuit Teal #2B6E68 — cool mineral ground, the coldest terrain in the set
- SECONDARY (~18%): Slate Gunmetal #374350 — the rock the crystal grew out of
- TERTIARY (~7%): Bone White #F2EDDF — refractive vein highlights along facet edges,
  matte, never glowing
- TEXTURE REGISTER: faceted and crystalline. Flat mineral planes meeting at sharp
  angles, veins running in branching lines. Angular like the junkyard but MINERAL and
  cool rather than manufactured and rusted — that is the distinction the player reads.

IMPORTANT — DO NOT USE THE VOLT ELEMENT COLOUR. Volt cyan #2FE8E8 is reserved for
element identity on parts, glyphs and ability FX. A ground surface wearing it would
read as an element marker. This terrain is Volt-ADJACENT in story, expressed through
the cooler Circuit Teal, never through the element accent. No Lightning Fork glyph.
No emissive glow anywhere on the ground.

WORLD PALETTE ONLY: Circuit Teal #2B6E68, Slate Gunmetal #374350, Bone White #F2EDDF
(sparse). No Ironmoss Green. No Harvest Crimson.

CRITICAL — THE PREVIOUS DELIVERY FAILED ON THESE TWO. Read them twice:

1. FULLY OPAQUE. Every pixel at alpha 255. The last ground tile shipped at alpha
   70-120 with zero fully-opaque pixels, so it blended with the dark background and
   rendered as grey mud instead of coloured ground. No transparency anywhere, no soft
   alpha edges, no semi-transparent overlay layer.

2. LOW-FREQUENCY, LOW-CONTRAST. The last tile shipped as a high-contrast checkerboard
   which read as visual noise competing with the character. Required instead: broad
   soft patches, gentle variation, the eye should slide over it. Keep the lightest and
   darkest pixel CLOSE in value. If the tile is interesting to look at on its own, it
   is wrong — it is a stage, not a subject (art-bible 3.5, 6.2).

SEAMLESS TILING: all four edges tile without a seam. The tile must hold up as a 40x26
grid without the repeat becoming visible — avoid any single distinctive element that
the eye can lock onto and count.

GREYSCALE TEST: reads as an even mid-tone surface with no element the eye catches.

NEGATIVE: No bot sprites. No UI chrome. No element glyphs on the ground. No glyphed
amber (art-bible 4.1 matte-amber discipline). No transparency. No high contrast. No
pixel-noise stippling. No photoreal. No PBR. No baked lighting or drop shadow. No
visible tile border.
