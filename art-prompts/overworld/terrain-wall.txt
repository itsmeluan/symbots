Overworld obstacle tile. Canvas 64x64 px. Top-down, seamlessly tileable.

This tile is the ONE EXCEPTION to the terrain set's recede-into-the-background rule,
and the exception is functional rather than aesthetic: a wall the player cannot read
as a wall is a wall they walk into repeatedly. It must be UNAMBIGUOUSLY DISTINCT from
all four walkable terrains at a glance — the player has to know where they can go
without testing it.

It still must not out-compete the character. The way to be readable without shouting:
distinguish by VALUE and EDGE, not by hue saturation or busy detail. A wall is darker,
harder-edged and flatter than any ground. It does not need colour to be obvious.

CONTENT:
- PRIMARY: Slate Gunmetal #374350 — structural rock and poured plate
- SECONDARY (~15%): Bone White #F2EDDF — exposed structural frame along the top
  facing edge, matte, reading as the lit rim of a raised mass
- TERTIARY (~8%): Alloy Ochre #C49A35 — mineral staining, matte, sparse
- TEXTURE REGISTER: solid, blocky, planar. Reads as MASS — something with height that
  the character is looking at the top and near side of, not a flat floor patch.

HEIGHT READ IS THE WHOLE JOB: from directly overhead, "solid" comes from a defined
top face plus a suggestion of thickness at the lower edge. A slightly lighter top face
and a darker band along the bottom edge is enough — a hard stepped transition, never a
gradient (art-bible 1: no soft shading, stepped ramps only).

SEAMLESS TILING: walls are painted in runs and blocks, so the tile must tile against
ITSELF cleanly in every direction — horizontal runs, vertical runs, and solid filled
areas must all look deliberate. Avoid a design that only reads correctly as a single
isolated tile.

FULLY OPAQUE: every pixel at alpha 255. No transparency, no soft alpha edge. A wall
tile that is partly transparent shows the void behind the map.

WORLD PALETTE ONLY: Slate Gunmetal #374350, Bone White #F2EDDF, Alloy Ochre #C49A35.
No Ironmoss Green, no Circuit Teal (those read as walkable ground). No Harvest Crimson
— crimson is hazard, and a wall is an obstacle, not a threat.

GREYSCALE TEST: in greyscale the wall must still be clearly separable from all four
ground tiles by value alone. This is the test that matters most for this asset — if it
only reads as a wall in colour, colourblind players walk into it.

NEGATIVE: No bot sprites. No UI chrome. No element glyphs. No transparency. No
gradients or soft shading. No baked drop shadow onto neighbouring ground (the tile
does not know what is next to it). No photoreal. No PBR. No rounded corners.
