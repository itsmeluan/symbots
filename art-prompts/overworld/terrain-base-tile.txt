Overworld tile: Base Terrain. Canvas 64x64 px. Top-down, seamlessly tileable.

The ground surface tile that fills the overworld map. This is the world's
"stage" — it must recede behind Symbots and the player sprite. Per art-bible §3.5
and §6.2: the environment must be LOW-FREQUENCY (broad shapes, gentle gradients,
sparse high-detail accents) so Symbots read as figure against it.

CONTENT: A matte, stylized ground surface combining the mechanical wilderness:
- PRIMARY BASE: Ironmoss Green #3D7A4A — the dominant ground color (overgrown
  mechanical wilderness, vegetation cover as the baseline)
- SECONDARY ACCENT: Alloy Ochre #C49A35 at ~20% coverage — dry earth or stone
  patches showing through the vegetation
- TERTIARY: Circuit Teal #2B6E68 at ~5% — exposed circuitry running like roots
  through the ground surface (the world is part machine)
- TEXTURE CHARACTER: low-frequency organic texture — broad soft patches, no
  pixel-noise. The texture reads as "ground" at any zoom level, not wallpaper.
  Think: gentle variations in a matte painted surface, not pixel-art stippling.

SEAMLESS TILING REQUIREMENT:
All four edges must tile without visible seam lines. Use symmetrical or offset
noise patterns that avoid regular repetition at the tile boundary.
- No hard edges at tile boundaries
- No obviously repeating detail pattern (Pokémon-style tiling avoidance)
- The tile must hold up for a 12x8 grid of tiles without looking like a grid

LOW-FREQUENCY REQUIREMENT (art-bible §3.5):
- No high-contrast detail — the ground never competes with the Symbot silhouette
- Maximum contrast within the tile: the difference between the lightest and
  darkest pixel should be LOW (the tile is low-contrast ground)
- No individual elements (plants, rocks, wires) that are visually prominent enough
  to catch the eye without a bot nearby

WORLD PALETTE ONLY: Ironmoss Green #3D7A4A, Alloy Ochre #C49A35, Circuit Teal #2B6E68.
No other colors. No Harvest Crimson (hazard), no Bone White (structural highlights),
no element colors on the ground surface.

GREYSCALE TEST: In greyscale the tile reads as a mid-tone matte ground surface —
no high-contrast elements, even ground saturation.

NEGATIVE: No bot sprites. No UI chrome. No Flame Chevron glyph or other element
glyphs on the ground. No matte amber surfaces with element markers (art-bible §4.1
matte-amber discipline: amber on ground is NEVER glyphed). No photoreal. No PBR.
No hard tile edges. No baked lighting or baked shadow.
