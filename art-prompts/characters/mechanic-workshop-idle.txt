Human mechanic/engineer at a workbench in idle pose, close-camera portrait-scale framing.
Canvas 256x256 px. 3/4 view character portrait perspective.

CHARACTER DESCRIPTION:
The same engineer as the overworld sprite, but now the camera is close enough to read
clothing and face detail. Seated or standing at the edge of a workbench, mid-assembly
posture — arms positioned as if adjusting a part, relaxed but focused body language.
Calm, unhurried expression. The workbench surface is implied (lower edge of frame) but
the Symbot itself is NOT in this asset (it is composited separately in the game).

POSE: Focused idle — one hand resting on bench, other positioned as if reaching for a
tool or part. Weight distributed evenly, no urgency in the stance. Think: "a craftsperson
pausing to examine their work." Body language conveys "this is my lab."

LIGHTING: Warm, even indoor bench-lamp light from above and slightly front. High ambient,
low directional shadow. No dramatic rim light, no mysterious shadows. The scene feels
like a well-lit garage workshop — every detail visible, warm but not yellow-orange.
Workshop palette: warm-neutral indoor tones, parts and clothing read at true color.

PALETTE: Same world-palette clothing as overworld sprite (earth tones, muted, lower
saturation than any robot). Flat indexed color regions for palette-swap compatibility.
At this scale, face reads — keep expression calm and professional, not heroic or flashy.

GREYSCALE TEST: In greyscale at 128px the pose reads as a focused human at work.

NEGATIVE: No Symbot in frame. No visible robot parts being held (hands empty or implied
reach). No dramatic shadow. No high-saturation neon. No weapons. No combat armor. No
photoreal. No painterly gradients. No baked gradients. No HUD elements.
