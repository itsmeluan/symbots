Human field engineer/mechanic walking sprite for a top-down 2.5D overworld map.
Canvas 64x96 px per frame. Top-down 2.5D Pokémon-style perspective.

CHARACTER DESCRIPTION:
A practical, tool-forward field mechanic — the engineer archetype, not a soldier or mage.
Wears layered work clothing: a durable jacket or coverall in muted earth tones, tool pouch
or satchel visible at the hip, practical boots. No weapons, no armor, no heroic cape.
Two variants sharing the same silhouette footprint:
- Masculine: slightly broader shoulders, close-cropped or short hair
- Feminine: same jacket/coverall silhouette, slightly narrower shoulder, medium or pulled-back hair
Both read as "engineer who works in the field among machines."

PALETTE: World palette clothing — warm earthy muted tones from Ironmoss Green (#3D7A4A),
Alloy Ochre (#C49A35), Slate Gunmetal (#374350), Bone White (#F2EDDF). Lower saturation
than any robot on screen. Matte surfaces, flat indexed color regions (no gradient — must
support palette-swap shader).

POSE: 4-directional walk cycle, 4 frames each:
- Down (facing camera): arms swing slightly, legs alternate, facing slightly toward viewer
- Up (walking away): back visible, same gait
- Left / Right: side profile walk, clean silhouette
Priority is readable facing direction and clear "human, not robot" read at small scale.
Spend detail on walk-cycle silhouette, NOT facial detail (too small to read at map zoom).

GREYSCALE TEST: In greyscale at 64x96px the sprite reads as a human figure moving with
direction — not a bot, not a UI element.

NEGATIVE: No weapons. No armor. No glowing robot parts. No visible CORE sphere. No robots
in frame. No HUD elements. No high saturation neon. No rounded-corner UI. No photoreal.
No baked gradients. No high-detail face (too small to matter).
