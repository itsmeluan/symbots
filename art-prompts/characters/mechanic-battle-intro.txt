Human mechanic in a confident battle-intro cameo pose. Canvas 256x256 px. 3/4 view,
trainer-style battle entrance framing.

CHARACTER DESCRIPTION:
The same engineer, but in a "here we go" entrance moment — the trainer-style beat at
the start of a battle before the mechanic yields the frame to the Symbot that actually
fights. Slightly more dynamic energy than the Workshop idle, but this is still a
person-not-a-fighter: confident engineer stance, not a combat stance. Like a Pokémon
trainer throwing their arm forward to send out their creature.

POSE: One arm extended or pointing forward (sending the Symbot out), other arm at side
or slightly behind for balance. Weight forward, chin up, calm confident expression.
Not aggressive — purposeful and decisive. The pose says "I built this thing, let's go."

LIGHTING: Slightly more dramatic than Workshop — this is the moment before combat so
the lighting transitions toward the combat mood. Warm light on the character, clean
background (no scene behind them — they appear against the battle screen or a neutral
gradient area). The lighting makes them read clearly without competing with the Symbot
they are about to yield the frame to.

PALETTE: Same world-palette clothing. Slightly higher contrast than Workshop idle
(to read at the battle screen scale against a desaturated combat backdrop). Still
matte, still flat indexed colors, still lower saturation than any Symbot in frame.

GREYSCALE TEST: In greyscale at 128px the pose reads as a human in a forward-reaching
confident stance.

NEGATIVE: No Symbot in frame (they are absent from this sprite; rendered separately).
No health bar, no stats display, no targeting reticle. No weapons drawn. No combat armor.
No high-saturation neon. No photoreal. No painterly gradients. No dramatic explosions.
